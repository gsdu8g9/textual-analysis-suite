DLXX:V:0. In the place of the cross the indivisible point which hath no points nor parts nor magnitude. Nor indeed hath it position, being beyond space. Nor hath it existence in time, for it is beyond Time. Nor hath it cause or effect, seeing that its Universe is infinite every way, and partaketh not of these our conceptions.

DLXX:V:2. Nor was he ashamed, hearing the laughter of the little dogs of hell. 
DLXX:V:3. For he abode in his place, and his falsehood was truth in his place. 
DLXX:V:4. The little dogs cannot correct him, for they can do naught but bark.

DLXX:V:7. Yea, even I the man behold this wonder. 
DLXX:V:8. And I could not deliver it unto myself. 
DLXX:V:9. That which established me is invisible and unknowable in its essence. 
DLXX:V:10. Only they who know IT may be known. 
DLXX:V:11. For they have the genius of the mighty sword 418.
