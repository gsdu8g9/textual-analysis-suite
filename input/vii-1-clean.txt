VII:I:1. My God, how I love Thee! 
VII:I:2. With the vehement appetite of a beast I hunt Thee through the Universe. 
VII:I:3. Thou art standing as it were upon a pinnacle at the edge of some fortified city. I am a white bird, and perch upon Thee. 
VII:I:4. Thou art My Lover: I see Thee as a nymph with her white limbs stretched by the spring. 
VII:I:5. She lies upon the moss; there is none other but she: 
VII:I:6. Art Thou not Pan? 
VII:I:7. I am He. Speak not, O my God! Let the work be accomplished in silence. 
VII:I:8. Let my cry of pain be crystallized into a little white fawn to run away into the forest! 
VII:I:9. Thou art a centaur, O my God, from the violet-blossoms that crown Thee to the hoofs of the horse. 
VII:I:10. Thou art harder than tempered steel; there is no diamond beside Thee. 
VII:I:11. Did I not yield this body and soul? 
VII:I:12. I woo thee with a dagger drawn across my throat. 
VII:I:13. Let the spout of blood quench Thy blood-thirst, O my God! 
VII:I:14. Thou art a little white rabbit in the burrow Night. 
VII:I:15. I am greater than the fox and the hole. 
VII:I:16. Give me Thy kisses, O Lord God! 
VII:I:17. The lightning came and licked up the little flock of sheep. 
VII:I:18. There is a tongue and a flame; I see that trident walking over the sea. 
VII:I:19. A phoenix hath it for its head; below are two prongs. They spear the wicked. 
VII:I:20. I will spear Thee, O Thou little grey god, unless Thou beware! 
VII:I:21. From the grey to the gold; from the gold to that which is beyond the gold of Ophir. 
VII:I:22. My God! but I love Thee! 
VII:I:23. Why hast Thou whispered so ambiguous things? Wast Thou afraid, O goat-hoofed One, O horned One, O pillar of lightning? 
VII:I:24. From the lightning fall pearls; from the pearls black specks of nothing. 
VII:I:25. I based all on one, one on naught. 
VII:I:26. Afloat in the fther, O my God, my God! 
VII:I:27. O Thou great hooded sun of glory, cut off these eyelids! 
VII:I:28. Nature shall die out; she hideth me, closing mine eyelids with fear, she hideth me from My destruction, O Thou open eye. 
VII:I:29. O ever-weeping One! 
VII:I:30. Not Isis my mother, nor Osiris my self; but the incestuous Horus given over to Typhon, so may I be! 
VII:I:31. There thought; and thought is evil. 
VII:I:32. Pan! Pan! Io Pan! it is enough. 
VII:I:33. Fall not into death, O my soul! Think that death is the bed into which you are falling! 
VII:I:34. O how I love Thee, O my God! Especially is there a vehement parallel light from infinity, vilely diffracted in the haze of this mind. 
VII:I:35. I love Thee. I love Thee. I love Thee. 
VII:I:36. Thou art a beautiful thing whiter than a woman in the column of this vibration. 
VII:I:37. I shoot up vertically like an arrow, and become that Above. 
VII:I:38. But it is death, and the flame of the pyre. 
VII:I:39. Ascend in the flame of the pyre, O my soul! Thy God is like the cold emptiness of the utmost heaven, into which thou radiatest thy little light. 
VII:I:40. When Thou shall know me, O empty God, my flame shall utterly expire in Thy great N.O.X. 
VII:I:41. What shalt Thou be, my God, when I have ceased to love Thee? 
VII:I:42. A worm, a nothing, a niddering knave! 
VII:I:43. But Oh! I love Thee. 
VII:I:44. I have thrown a million flowers from the basket of the Beyond at Thy feet, I have anointed Thee and Thy Staff with oil and blood and kisses. 
VII:I:45. I have kindled Thy marble into life -- ay! into death. 
VII:I:46. I have been smitten with the reek of Thy mouth, that drinketh never wine but life. 
VII:I:47. How the dew of the Universe whitens the lips! 
VII:I:48. Ah! trickling flow of the stars of the mother Supernal, begone! 
VII:I:49. I Am She that should come, the Virgin of all men. 
VII:I:50. I am a boy before Thee, O Thou satyr God. 
VII:I:51. Thou wilt inflict the punishment of pleasure -- Now! Now! Now! 
VII:I:52. Io Pan! Io Pan! I love Thee. I love Thee. 
VII:I:53. O my God, spare me! 
VII:I:54. Now! It is done! Death. 
VII:I:55. I cried aloud the word -- and it was a mighty spell to bind the Invisible, an enchantment to unbind the bound; yea, to unbind the bound.
